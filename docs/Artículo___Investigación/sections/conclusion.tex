\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
% =============================================================

We presented a graph neural network framework for satellite-based solar
irradiance forecasting that addresses the situational awareness requirements
of resilient cyber-physical energy systems (CPES) operating under tropical
renewable uncertainty. The key contributions are: (i) a weighted $k$-NN
GraphSAGE-LSTM architecture that explicitly models spatial cloud structure
from GOES-16 patches; (ii) a rigorous comparative evaluation against
ResNet-LSTM, SARIMA, and persistence at two Colombian sites with contrasting
climatic regimes; and (iii) Split Conformal Prediction as a
distribution-free post-hoc UQ layer that transforms point forecasts into
coverage-guaranteed prediction intervals usable in risk-aware dispatch.

\textbf{GraphSAGE-LSTM} consistently outperforms ResNet-LSTM at the 6-hour horizon
for El~Paso ($\skillday = 0.636$ vs.\ $0.614$), with lower seed variance, confirming
that distance-weighted neighbourhood aggregation on the pixel graph provides a
more stable and expressive spatial representation than fixed convolutional filters
for this high-irradiance regime.

At the \textbf{tropical mountain site} (Uniandes), SARIMAX on the clear-sky index
achieves competitive or superior skill at 6~hours ($0.435$) relative to
GraphSAGE-LSTM ($0.417$), revealing that the strong diurnal autocorrelation of
the Bogot{\'a} clear-sky index limits the marginal benefit of satellite spatial
features at longer horizons. This finding has a direct CPES implication:
model selection should be climate-aware, and lightweight statistical baselines
should be included in any resilience-oriented benchmarking suite.

At the \textbf{1-hour horizon}, no model consistently beats persistence at El~Paso,
consistent with the low persistence error ($204.4$~W/m²) in a predominantly
clear-sky regime. Modest positive skill ($\approx 0.15$) is achieved at Uniandes,
where frequent cloud passages create exploitable structure.

\textbf{Future work} follows a staged uncertainty-quantification roadmap for
CPES resilience.  Split Conformal Prediction has been implemented as Stage~1;
Conformalized Quantile Regression \cite{romano2019} will yield adaptive-width
intervals that contract during predictable clear-sky conditions and expand
during uncertain cloud events (Stage~2), enabling tighter reserve scheduling.
Stage~3 will achieve full Bayesian decomposition of forecast uncertainty:
Gaussian variance heads will capture aleatoric uncertainty (irreducible cloud
stochasticity), while Stochastic Gradient Langevin Dynamics (SGLD,
\cite{welling2011}) will sample the posterior weight distribution to provide
epistemic uncertainty estimates \cite{kendall2017}, completing the transition
from point forecasts to risk-stratified probabilistic outputs for resilient
CPES dispatch.
Additional planned work includes the FlatMLP spatial-ablation analysis,
multi-step joint-horizon outputs, and extension to further Latin American sites
to build a broader tropical CPES forecasting benchmark.
